Second: Blood types

How many blood types are there ?

There are 8 main blood types, determined based on two systems:

The First:
The ABO system (depends on the presence of A or B antigens on the surface of red blood cells)

Blood Type Antigens
Type A Has A antigens
Type B Has B antigens
Type AB Has Both A and B antigens
Type O Has neither A nor B antigens

The Second:
The Rhesus factor (Rh) (an additional protein either present or absent
they call it positive or negative)

Rhesus Factor Protein
Positive   + The protein is present
Negative   - The protein is absent

Universal donor: O-

-There are no antigens (no A, no B, no Rh) on the red blood cells, so the recipient's immune system does not recognize them as foreign.

Universal recipient: AB+

Their body does not produce antibodies against A or B, so they can receive from all blood types.

A small piece of information about the
role of data science in core blood

1-Forecasting demand:-

Using historical data to predict future needs for blood types based on seasons, holidays, and emergencies.

Impact: Prevents blood shortages during critical periods.

2-Donor retention and engagement analytics:-

Analyzing donor behavior to identify regular donors and send personalized notifications when they become eligible again (every 3-4 months).

Impact: Building a loyal donor community and reducing emergency campaigns.

3-Inventory optimization and waste reduction:-

Smart algorithms to manage the life cycle of blood products. Platelets expire within 5 days, and red blood cells within 42 days.

Impact: Reducing medical waste and ensuring every donation is used effectively.

4-Donor eligibility prediction:-

Predictive models that assess the donor's health profile (age, weight, previous hemoglobin) to determine eligibility.

Impact: Improving user experience and reducing on-site deferrals.
