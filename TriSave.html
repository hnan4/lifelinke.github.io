How is donated blood used?

After donation, blood is separated into its main components:

  • Red blood cells →
    • tretat anemia and iron deficiency, save patients with severe bleeding from accidents and surgeries, support kidney and liver patients, and are essential for heart patients to improve oxygen levels.
  • White blood cells →
    • strengthen immunity after chemotherapy or radiation, are used in stem cell research to treat leukemia, and support patients with acquired or inherited immune deficiencies.
  • Platelets →
    • treat cancer patients who suffer from bleeding due to treatment, are essential in organ transplants and major surgeries, treat platelet deficiencies, and protect patients from dangerous internal bleeding.
  • Plasma →
    • treats hemophilia patients (recurrent bleeding), supports liver patients who have failed to produce clotting factors, and is essential for treating severe burns.

The story of a young man — On his way to university, a terrible accident. He entered the hospital unconscious. Internal bleeding almost ended his life within hours. 🩸 Real numbers from around the world:

· More than 118 million blood donations are collected every year worldwide.
· But still, in many countries, people die because blood is not available.
· In poor countries, more than half of all blood transfusions go to children under 5 — very young children who can die from simple illnesses if there is no blood.

•♡These are not movie stories — these are real stories from hospital wards we see every day.

🩸 Real numbers from Egypt:

· Road accidents and childbirth are the two biggest reasons for needing blood in Egypt.
· On holidays and public holidays, the blood supply drops dramatically, and many families struggle to find donors.
· Also during Ramadan, blood banks need more donors because some people donate less due to fasting.
